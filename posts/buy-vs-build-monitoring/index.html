Buy vs Build Monitoring

July 28, 2023

    Monitoring a network of any reasonable size, to the extent where failures can be accurately detected, is a non-trivial task. To some extent, the monitoring component could be more complex than the functionality and operation of the network itself once you consider external impacting factors.

    Monitoring can be one of those things that can be made as complex as you want it to be and can take into account as many factors as you want. If we’re in the business of operating a network for customer consumption, then monitoring becomes a necessary complexity.

    One approach to dealing with necessary complexity is to abstract it away by buying a vendor product. This gives the consumer a blackbox that “does what it says on the tin” and a vendor to blame when the system doesn’t perform. The downside being, you will never get a 100% fit for features and functionality to fit your business, your customers or your network - there will always be compromises and the chosen product will be “good enough”. E.g. ease of use versus feature depth or initial configuration time vs level of customisation.

    An alternative approach is to build a monitoring system that best means the business needs. While it might not have the snazzy UI and a one-click MONITOR ALL THE THINGS approach, it can deliver high quality, contextualised information about the operation of your network. By context, I mean additional information that’s specific to your business and network to add additional meaning to monitoring signals. Greater meaning means higher quality, actionable alerts that can be handled without the need for deep network tribal knowledge.
